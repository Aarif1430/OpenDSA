An investor places N,000 into a stock fund. 10 years later the account has a value of M,000. Using logarithms and antilogarithms, determine the average annual growth rate for this fund.(Round to the nearest two digits)

1.15

If you start with N and in 10 years you get M, then you want to solve for X in the equation N * X^10 = M.

You will need to use logs/antilogs. Fortunately, you can use whatever base you want for the logarithm. Since many calculators will calculate the natural log (base e) and will also give you e^x, this might be the easiest base to work with.

Solving for X gives us: 10 ln X = ln M/N ln X = (ln M/N)/10 X = e^((ln M/N)/10)
